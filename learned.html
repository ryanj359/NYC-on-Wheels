What I've Learned

After living here four years, give or take, I've learned a lot about this city. I've learned which streets I prefer to cross at. I've learned the parks I love to walk through. I've learned which bus routes get me where I need to go, and which ones say they'll show up and never do. I've learned how to be alone, and how to be okay with being alone. I've simultaneously learned that you're never really alone here. I've learned that talking to strangers can be the most interesting part of your month. I've learned that knowing the bar tenders and bouncers at the bar down the street can almost make it feel like you live in a doorman building, at least on the weekends. I've learned that getting to know your local deli guy or barista will give you a sense of home. I've learned that having support is one of the best feelings in the world, and so is knowing someone has yours. I've learned that dating is the worst, but fun when you can laugh about it with your friends. I've learned that my people means everything to me. And from this project, I've learned that community is one of the most necessary things in life.
